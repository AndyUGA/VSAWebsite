Executive Board Elections (2020/2021)
February 6th

Interested in taking up a leadership role in VSA? Read this page to see what positions would be the right fit for you and prepare yourself for the upcoming elections!

Elections will take place in early February of 2020 based on scheduling and availability of the voting general body.

Contact Summer Phung, the 2020 Elections Moderator, with any questions about the election process!

Rules / Eligibility
How to be Eligible
  • Must be a student at the University of Georgia through the upcoming fall semester .
  • Must have attended at least two general body meetings or events during the past term.
  • Indication to slate down to any position must be made BEFORE opposing candidates begin any speeches (i.e. during the nomination process) and they must have listed the position as one of interest on their application. Entering the race following another candidate’s speech will not be permitted.
  • Completion of executive board application must be done by Sunday, February 2nd, at 11:59 p.m. ET .
  • However, you may choose to retract your application at any time if you choose not to run.
Apply Here!
How to Nominate
  • Nominations are due Friday, January 31st, at 11:59 p.m.
  • A nomination is NOT required for a member to participate in elections.
  • The Elections moderator will reach out to the nominee to either accept or reject the nomination prior to the application due date.
Nominate Today!
How to be Elected
  • A majority of voting members present at the time of elections
  • A member qualifies for voting privileges if they have attended at least 2 VSA GBMS or EVENTS
  • Candidate must receive a majority vote by anonymous ballot
  • In the event of a tie, the exiting Executive Board will deliberate and vote.ate and vote.
Election Process

Rules

  • All positions will be listed on the board
  • Moderator will call out the names of those who completed the application and confirm whether they would like to continue in elections.
  • In the event of a candidate’s loss, they are given the option to slate down to another position of interest that they indicated on their application. Those that slate must announce their intention to run BEFORE the first nominee of that position speaks.
  • Elections will be facilitated by one or two neutral parties who have given up their voting rights to ensure a fair and smooth election process.
  • Timing is subect to change based on number of candidates running

Format

  • The order of elections will be: President, Internal Vice President, External Vice President, Treasurer, Secretary, Public Relations, and Historian.
  • Each candidate will get 3 minutes to speak and will be cut off if they go over
  • For President, each nominee gets 5 minutes to speak
  • Following candidates' speeches, there will be a brief Q&A section where questions may be asked by the voting body. ALL questions must pertain to ALL of the candidates and cannot be specified to only one.
  • The first two questions will be selected prior to elections and asked by the moderator.
  • The last two questions will be asked by members of the general body.

Voting

  • Following Q&A, candidates will be asked to leave the room and a deliberation period of 10 to 15 minutes will begin.
  • After deliberation, every member gets a sheet of paper to vote anonymously.
  • After deliberation, every member gets a sheet of paper to vote anonymously. Results will be announced immediately after the counting process finishes.
  • In the event that a nominee does not get the position they ran for, they have the option to slate down and run for another position.
